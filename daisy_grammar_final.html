Part D：Simple or Compound Sentence?
S = Simple sentence (one idea). C = Compound sentence (two ideas joined by and, but, or or with a comma).
Question 18 · 2.5 pts
My uncle has a farm.
S — Simple
C — Compound
✔ One idea, no conjunction. This is a Simple sentence.
Question 19 · 2.5 pts
Mom watered the plants, and Dad cut the grass.
S — Simple
C — Compound
✔ Two ideas joined by and with a comma. This is a Compound sentence.
Question 20 · 2.5 pts
Grandpa feeds the sheep.
S — Simple
C — Compound
✔ One idea, no conjunction. This is a Simple sentence.
Question 21 · 2.5 pts
We can pick berries, or we can milk the cow.
S — Simple
C — Compound
✔ Two choices joined by or with a comma. This is a Compound sentence.
Question 22 · 2.5 pts
My sister is full, but she still wants more milk.
S — Simple
C — Compound
✔ Two contrasting ideas joined by but with a comma. This is a Compound sentence.
Question 23 · 2.5 pts
Danny plays the piano every day.
S — Simple
C — Compound
✔ One idea, no conjunction. This is a Simple sentence.

Part H：Prepositions
Place: at (school, home, park) · on (surfaces) · in (inside). Direction: above, beside, near, around, over, under

Question 39 · 2.5 pts

✔ The sun is higher than the boy's head. Use above to mean higher than something.
